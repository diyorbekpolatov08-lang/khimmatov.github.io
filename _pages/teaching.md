## 👨‍🏫 Teaching Overview

I am an Assistant at the Institute of Nuclear Technologies, Department of Nuclear Physics and Astronomy, Samarkand State University (since 2026). I deliver lectures, practical sessions, and laboratory classes across nine courses covering fundamental and applied nuclear physics. I also supervise student research projects and contribute to laboratory infrastructure development and gamma-spectrometric measurements.

---

## 📚 Courses Taught

### General Physics
**Type:** Core lecture course | **Level:** Undergraduate

Introduction to the fundamental principles of classical and modern physics, including mechanics, thermodynamics, electromagnetism, optics, and quantum phenomena.

---

### Physics – Part 2
**Type:** Core lecture & practical | **Level:** Undergraduate

Continuation of the general physics curriculum, covering electrodynamics, oscillations, waves, and introductory quantum and nuclear physics.

---

### Physics Practicum (Nuclear Physics)
**Type:** Laboratory course | **Level:** Undergraduate

Hands-on laboratory experiments in nuclear physics including radiation detection, counting statistics, absorption measurements, and gamma spectrometry.

---

### Physics of Atomic Nucleus and Elementary Particles
**Type:** Lecture course | **Level:** Undergraduate

Structure of atomic nuclei, nuclear models, radioactive decay, nuclear reactions, fission and fusion, and introduction to elementary particle physics.

---

### Fundamentals of Astronomy and Astrophysics
**Type:** Lecture course | **Level:** Undergraduate

Overview of modern astronomy: celestial mechanics, stellar physics, galactic structure, and cosmological models.

---

### Methods of Recording Nuclear Radiation *(Elective)*
**Type:** Lecture & laboratory | **Level:** Undergraduate / Graduate

Principles of nuclear radiation detectors: ionization chambers, scintillation detectors, semiconductor detectors, and modern digital spectrometry systems. Calibration methods and data analysis.

---

### Production of Radioisotopes and Their Application in Medical Diagnostics and Therapy *(Elective)*
**Type:** Lecture course | **Level:** Graduate

Radioisotope production via reactors and accelerators; applications in PET, SPECT, targeted radionuclide therapy, and brachytherapy. Overview of current clinical radiopharmaceuticals.

---

### Elements of Microworld Physics *(Elective)*
**Type:** Lecture course | **Level:** Undergraduate

Quantum mechanics, wave-particle duality, atomic structure, and introductory nuclear and particle physics for non-specialist students.

---

### Physics of Brachytherapy and Proton Therapy *(Elective)*
**Type:** Lecture course | **Level:** Graduate

Physical principles of modern radiotherapy modalities: brachytherapy source dosimetry, proton beam physics, Bragg peak, treatment planning concepts, and radiation protection in clinical settings.

---

## 🧪 Laboratory & Supervision

- Gamma-spectrometric laboratory measurements and student training
- Supervision of undergraduate and graduate research projects in nuclear physics and dosimetry
- Development and maintenance of laboratory infrastructure
- Data processing and scientific report preparation with students
