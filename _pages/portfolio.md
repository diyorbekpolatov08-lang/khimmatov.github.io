## 🔬 Research Overview

My research is focused on the intersection of nuclear physics instrumentation and medical applications — specifically developing and validating gamma-ray spectrometric systems for dosimetry in neutron capture therapy (NCT). I combine experimental measurements with Monte Carlo simulations (GEANT4, MCNP) to model radiation transport and dose deposition in biological and phantom targets.

---

## ☢️ Boron Neutron Capture Therapy (BNCT) Dosimetry

The central theme of my PhD research:

- Development of a **xenon gamma spectrometric complex** for absorbed dose determination in boron-containing targets irradiated with thermal neutrons
- Experimental validation on the **medical beam of the IRT MEPhI research reactor** (Moscow)
- Dose load determination in NCT using high-pressure xenon gamma-ray spectrometers
- Comparison with conventional dosimetry methods

**Dissertation:** *Gamma-Ray Spectrometric Complex for Determining Absorbed Dose in Boron-Containing Targets Irradiated with Thermal Neutrons*
Defended at: **NRNU MEPhI** (National Research Nuclear University, Moscow)
Supervisor: **Prof. S.E. Ulin**

---

## ⚗️ Gadolinium Neutron Capture Therapy (GdNCT)

Extension of NCT research to gadolinium-based agents:

- GEANT4 Monte Carlo simulations for GdNCT dose modeling
- Optimization of Gd-157 mediated neutron capture therapy parameters
- Comparison of BNCT and GdNCT dosimetric characteristics

---

## 💻 Monte Carlo Simulations

High-fidelity radiation transport modeling:

- **GEANT4** simulations for NCT geometry, neutron beam modeling, and dose calculation
- **MCNP** for neutron transport in reactor beam configurations and shielding problems
- **ROOT (CERN)** for spectral data analysis and visualization
- Simulation of xenon gamma spectrometer response functions

Experimental and instrumentation work:

- Operation and calibration of HPGe and xenon gamma spectrometers
- Spectral analysis and radionuclide identification
- Radioecological measurements: soil, water, and environmental samples
- Application of gamma spectrometry in nuclear medicine diagnostics
